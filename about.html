The institute’s foundation stone was laid by Pandit Jawaharlal Nehru on October 10, 1959, beginning its journey in technical education and research excellence. Originally known as Regional Engineering College, Warangal, it received deemed university status and was renamed as NIT Warangal in 2002. NIT Warangal has played a pioneering role in engineering education in India, offering courses in various branches since inception, and evolving into a full-fledged university with advanced programs and research centers. Campus and Facilities Spread across 248 acres, the campus houses modern academic buildings, labs, hostels, sports complexes, student activity centers, library, shopping center, and dedicated research centers. The campus promotes innovation and entrepreneurship through the Dr. VA Sastry Centre for Innovation and Incubation and a 24x7 Innovation Garage space for students. Academics and Achievements The institute has thirteen departments covering engineering, sciences, and management, with over 6,000 students enrolled in undergraduate and postgraduate programs, in addition to over 1,000 research scholars. NIT Warangal consistently ranks among India’s top engineering institutes, known for its dedicated faculty, research achievements, and a vibrant student community. NIT Warangal stands out for its legacy, academic excellence, and modern campus, remaining a key destination for technical education and research in India. NIT Warangal is recognized as an Institute of National Importance by the Government of India, and ranks among the top engineering institutes in the country. The campus features modern infrastructure with ongoing upgrades, and houses centers for innovation, incubation, and research, fostering start-up activity and industry collaboration. A vibrant student life is showcased, including major annual technical, cultural, and management events such as Technozion, SpringSpree, and CURA, drawing thousands of participants from across India.
